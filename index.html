Sobie Don

Hi, I’m Sobie Don (Julie)! I’m a lifelong learner, researcher, and community advocate, dedicated to leveraging technology to solve real-world challenges and empowering those around me. I graduated from the University of Nebraska Omaha, earning my undergraduate degree in Information Systems, followed by dual master’s degrees in Business Administration (MBA) and Management Information Systems (MIS).

I am passionate about artificial intelligence (AI) and its potential to transform industries and improve everyday lives. I’m particularly fascinated by neural networks—their ability to mimic human brain functions in analyzing data and recognizing patterns—and how they can create smarter, adaptive systems. I am also passionate about advancing human-AI interaction, designing systems that work alongside humans to enhance decision-making, creativity, and efficiency in both personal and corporate environments.

Beyond my professional focus, I am committed to building strong communities. I co-founded Nebraska Women of Color in Tech, an organization that creates a sense of community, empowers women, and provides mentorship and career opportunities in tech. I also serve as the Co-Chair of the Professional Networking Committee at UPTIES, a Union Pacific Employee Resource Group (ERG), where I lead initiatives that support community organizations, such as College Possible, to create brighter futures for underserved students.

In addition to these roles, I coordinated the Tech Department at Union Pacific, helping to lead a company-wide fundraising effort that raised an impressive $950,000 for United Way.

When I’m not working, researching, or volunteering, I enjoy traveling, trying out new restaurants, and playing golf.
